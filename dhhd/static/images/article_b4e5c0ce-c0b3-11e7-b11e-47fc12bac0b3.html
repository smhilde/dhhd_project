Several new restaurants will be opening soon in Edmond providing more places for local residents to eat and spend time with friends.

  • Elisabeth Slay | The Edmond Sun
alert featured
What's for dinner

New restaurants opening throughout city

  • Elisabeth Slay | The Edmond Sun
  • Nov 3, 2017

Every day Edmond grows larger and in the near future there will be several new restaurants built and placed throughout, expanding the city's edible offerings.

Director of Edmond Economic Development Authority Janet Yowell said new establishments such as S&B Burgers and Old School Bagel Cafe will join Edmonds food industry.

What we keep is a list of everybody that has pulled a permit either for a new restaurant or a lot of times theyll go into existing space in town and we think thats wonderful because theyre utilizing our existing real estate, Yowell said.

Currently, a total of at least nine establishments are in the process of being built or taking over an existing space around the city.

In the upcoming months these new restaurants will open and residents can enjoy the convenience of some of their favorite places.

Those restaurants are: Taco Bell at 15 and Frontage Rd., Roxys Ice Cream Social at 1380 W. Covell Rd. #116, Zaxbys at Medical Center Dr. and Bryant Ave., Fait Maison at 152 E. 5 St., Hash Tag at 1501 N. Santa Fe #156, Braums Ice Cream at 2624 Market St., Pho Toan at 2137 W. Edmond Rd., S&B Burger Joint at 840 W. Danforth Rd., and Old School Bagel Cafe at 231 S. Coltrane.

As far as restaurants opening, its been a really strong year for that but were not seeing a lot of sit down restaurants that are looking in our market so were seeing more of a fast casual, Yowell said.

All of these new developments, Yowell said, are enabling the residents of Edmond to eat their meals at a variety of places perhaps closer to their home

They give us more choices to shop and spend our money within the city limits, Yowell said.

According to Yowell, the permit retrieval process for a new restaurant varies depending on location and construction development.

If youre just going to apply for an alteration permit it has to be that you're going to use a licensed contractor for work ... you have to pull permit, Yowell said.

Restaurant owners need permits for new construction and existing structures and depending on the development it can take 30 to 45 days to receive a permit.

Members of the community such as University of Central Oklahoma student, Morgan Flanagan, are excited to see the citys growth.

Being a college student. Im extremely excited about restaurants like S&Bs because it gives me more options in my own community instead of having to drive to (Oklahoma City) to eat, Flanagan said.

The resident also said she feels these new places will launch the city into popularity.

I think itll positively benefit the community by bringing people together for some good food and bringing outsiders into our community and putting Edmond on the map, Flanagan said.

For residents such as Sarah Murphy, the variety of establishments will not only provide more dining options but also provide employment opportunities.

I love food, so the more the merrier. It would also be nice to have more job opportunities in the community, Murphy said.

City officials expect Edmond to continue growing, allowing residents new and old to enjoy their favorite eateries in the place they call home.
